3-Year War­ranty

Why do we do that?

Because we stand by the products we offer. We're an authorized dealer for all the Top Brands and offer high quality own brand products as inexpensive alternatives. We take great care to offer quality products only. If a product doesn't meet our quality standards, we won't include it in our catalogue, no matter how cheap it is or how well that manufacturer's other products sell. Our quality control and our six service & repair workshops ensure the best possible quality for you and the fastest possible help in case of a problem. That's why we dare to extend the manufacturers' warranty period to three years.

Are there exceptions?

Signs of wear are only covered if they occurred despite appropriate care or because of demonstrably bad workmanship. In other words: even an expensive trumpet looks pretty bad after a while if you don't wipe off sweat and spit. We're very obliging, but neither we nor the manufacturer can be held responsible for inappropriate care :-) We deliver all instruments with basic care tips. For consumable items as well as products where the manufacturer offers more than 3 years' warranty, the manufacturer's warranty is still valid of course.

What if something is broken?

If something's broken, before you return it, please contact our customer service department. Our warranty is valid for all purchases since 1 March 2005. You don't need any papers except your invoice number (your customer number usually suffices, too). Please contact our customer service team in the first instance as they can advise the next best possible step.

What if something is beyond repair?

A product that is beyond repair will be exchanged for a new product. If the same model isn't available anymore, we'll offer you an alternative in the same price range, or a refund of the full amount you paid - that's right - even towards the end of our 3-year warranty! Naturally, this assumes proper usage of the item. :-)

Any questions?

...that since 2002 Music Shop has had its own dedicated department for quality control, which is concerned with nothing but enhancing the quality of our products? If a product doesn't meet our standards in terms of quality, workmanship and functionality, we won't put it in our catalogue. Our own brand products are continually improved in conjunction with their manufacturers, and even many renowned brand names have manufacturers come to rely on feedback from us in order our quality control department to improve their products.
